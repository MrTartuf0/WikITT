Sirio

The telephone was designed by Giugiaro Design in 1987. The project’s goal was to create a telephone with simple and elegant lines to meet the company's need for production on a large scale in order to replace its predecessor: the Pulsar model.


The “Sirio phone” is a push- button telephone produced by Brondi and FATME for the Italian Society Exercising Telecommunications (SIP) in the 1990s.


A switch placed on the back of the device allowed users to manually choose whether to dial the number with a DTMF tone or with a decadic pulse type of dialing.


It was the first landline telephone in Italy, designed to dial the number with dual tone multi-frequency (DTMF) technology.
